Axel Allétru

"What I am trying to demonstrate today, is that it is possible to put yourself back on track, maintain your level of demand, continue to dream and have ambitions"

After multiple European championship titles and vice-world junior BMX champion, Axel evolves and devotes himself to motocross. Professional driver, he is considered as a great hope of the French motocross, senior category. He climbed the national ladder to represent France at the MX2 World Championships. In 2010, at the World Championships in Latvia, Axel fell. His career will then take a turn. The verdict is without appeal: at 20 years, Axel is paraplegic. After months of immobilization, he began his rehabilitation.

Today and thanks to an unusual mind, he walks with crutches and stands up thanks to raisers keeping him the right angle ankles. He demonstrates his courage to continue to believe that it is possible.

The game he leads with life and with others has become a real wealth that he now shares with his audience. Real competitor, he considers different sports that would allow him to flourish despite his disability and pursue his career. I lay my sights on swimming. The medical staff at his rehabilitation center are impressed by his ease in the aquatic environment.

Step by step, he is rebuilding a champion’s life, swimming in high level, integrating the French Federation of Handisport Swimming.

In only three years, he becomes one of the best in its class. He trains in an Olympic swimming pool near Lille, a minimum of 15 hours a week, 6 days a week. Today, Axel is officially 12 times French Champion in the following categories : 50 meters freestyle, 100 meters freestyle, 50 meters butterfly, Vice Champion of Europe and french recordman on 50 and 100m freestyle.

" Would not a failure, an accident of life, be the chance of our lives? "
